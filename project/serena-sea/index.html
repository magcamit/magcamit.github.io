In the quest to understand In 2017, I travelled to Southeast Asia to visit and survey the locales where I will be conducting my Marie Curie research on the three-way nexus between security, religion, and nationalism. During my travel, I met several interesting people from Indonesia, Myanmar, and the Philippines, who shared their insights regarding the dynamics in the relationships between the members of the predominant and minority religious groups in their respective states. Beginning on September 1, 2019, I will be returning to these countries to conduct a three-month fieldwork in the following areas: Manila and Zamboanga City in the Philippines, Jakarta and West Papua in Indonesia, and Yangon and Mandalay in Myanmar. I will spend 30 days in each country to gather data from my elite and non-elite participants by conducting interviews and doing semi-ethnographic observations. In the meantime, I am writing my first journal manuscript for this project entitled, Why Nation-States Persist, and also doing the preliminary preparations for the fieldwork. I will keep you posted!
